# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary users are Finnish-speaking people choosing lunch in the Seinäjoki region. Most arrive on mobile around lunchtime and want to make a confident decision quickly; desktop remains an important secondary context.

The secondary user is the service's internal operator, who monitors source and refresh health, adds missing menu sources, and reviews recommendation calibration.

## Product Purpose

Mihin lounaalle? reduces daily choice overload by collecting the region's published lunch menus, distilling them into one shared top three, and keeping the full offering available for comparison. Success means a reader can confidently choose today's lunch in under a minute.

## Positioning

The fastest way to choose lunch around Seinäjoki—complete local menu coverage distilled into one transparent daily top three.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, local, and decisive. The product should speak in concise, natural Finnish and help people act without hype or unnecessary explanation.

## Anti-references

The product should not resemble a food-delivery marketplace, a generic SaaS dashboard, or an “AI-powered” novelty product.

## Design Principles

1. **Make the decision immediate.** Lead with today's strongest choices and keep the path from opening the service to choosing lunch short.
2. **Earn trust through evidence.** Explain the shared ranking criteria, identify sources and freshness, and communicate unavailable or stale data honestly.
3. **Keep the machinery backstage.** Make the recommendation useful and transparent without turning the underlying AI implementation into the product story.
4. **Design mobile first, not mobile only.** Prioritize fast lunchtime scanning on a phone while preserving a capable, composed desktop experience.
5. **Stay recognizably local.** Use direct Finnish language and a distinct local character without drifting into marketplace, SaaS, or technology-demo conventions.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Start with lightweight, general accessibility foundations appropriate to the responsive web product. Defer deeper accessibility requirements and a comprehensive implementation pass until the product's next dedicated accessibility review.
